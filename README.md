 ☕ Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Dashboard

A professional **Excel-based Data Visualization** project analyzing sales performance, customer footfall, and product trends for a multi-location coffee retail chain

📋 Project Overview
This dashboard transforms raw transactional data into actionable business insights. It allows stakeholders to track revenue growth, identify peak operational hours, and understand customer purchasing patterns across three major store locations: **Astoria**, **Hell's Kitchen**, and **Lower Manhattan**.

🚀 Key Features & Functionality
Dynamic Filtering:** Integrated **Excel Slicers** for "Month Name" and "Day Name" to allow users to drill down into specific timeframes.
KPI Tracking:** High-level metrics for Total Sales ($698K+), Total Footfall (149K+), and Average Bill per Person ($4.69).
Peak Hour Analysis:** A line chart visualizing "Quantity Ordered Based on Hours" to identify the morning rush (8:00 AM - 10:00 AM).
Product Performance:** Categorical breakdown showing that **Coffee (39%)** and **Tea (28%)** drive the majority of revenue.
Size Distribution:** Analysis showing that "Regular" and "Large" sizes dominate customer preference.

 🛠️ Technical Stack (Excel)
Power Query:Used for data cleaning, handling null values, and transforming date/time formats.
Pivot Tables: Leveraged to aggregate large datasets into summary tables for visualization.
Data Modeling: Created relationships between transaction logs and product categories.
Advanced Formulas: Utilized `SUMIFS`, `AVERAGEIFS`, and `XLOOKUP` for KPI calculations.
UI/UX Design: Custom color palette and layout optimization for a clean, professional "Cafe-themed" aesthetic.

 📈 Key Business Insights
1.  Morning Peak: Business volume peaks between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM, suggesting a need for maximum staffing during this window.
2.  Top Seller:Barista Espressois the highest-grossing product, generating over $91,400.
3.  Location Lead:Hell's Kitchen** leads in both total sales ($236,511) and footfall compared to other locations.
4.  Weekly Consistency: Sales remain remarkably stable throughout the work week, with Monday and Thursday showing peak performance.
